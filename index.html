Legal Answers to Common Questions

Who Can File a Wrongful Death Claim?

  • Spouse: If your spouse or domestic partner was the victim of wrongful death, you are legally entitled to file a wrongful death lawsuit and seek compensation for the loss. As the surviving spouse, you may be entitled to economic and non-economic damages.
  • Children: If you have lost your biological or adoptive parent, you are entitled to seek compensation. If you are the victim's stepchild and can prove financial dependence, you are also entitled to file for a wrongful death lawsuit.
  • Parents: If you are the parent of the deceased, you are entitled to file a wrongful death claim. This only applies if your child was a minor at the time of death or was an adult with no descendants.
  • Other Heirs: If no spouse, child, or parent is to be considered the survivor of the victim, another surviving family member may be found to be the “heir.” Heirs are people who can inherit the property of the deceased person legally and logically if the victim passed without a viable will in place.

How Long Do I Have to File a Wrongful Death Lawsuit?

California law dictates that survivors of a wrongful death victim have exactly two years following the incident to file a wrongful death suit in court. That is why it is so important for survivors of the decedent to contact a reputable law firm and file for a wrongful death lawsuit as soon as possible following the incident.

What can I recover for a wrongful death?

Losing someone you love is one of the hardest things anyone can go through. We know that no amount of money can ever make things right - but a wrongful death claim can help ease the financial stress and bring a sense of justice.

  • Financial support - If your loved one helped support you, we can help estimate what they would have earned in the future.
  • Lost benefits - This could include things like health insurance, retirement benefits, or other income they would have received.
  • Medical and funeral costs - These expenses can add up fast. A claim can help make sure you're not left to handle them alone.
  • Emotional loss - This includes the loss of love, companionship, guidance, and the emotional support your loved one gave you. These are often the hardest to talk about - but they matter, and the law recognizes that.

What can I recover for a catastrophic injury?

If you or someone you love has suffered a life-changing injury - like a spinal cord injury, traumatic brain injury, amputation, or severe burns - the impact can be overwhelming. Recovery isn't just about healing physically, it's also about getting the support you need to move forward emotionally, financially, and in everyday life.
